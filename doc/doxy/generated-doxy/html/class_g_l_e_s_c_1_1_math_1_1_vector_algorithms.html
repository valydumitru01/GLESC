inlinestatic

Computes the cross product of two vectors.

The cross product is a vector that is perpendicular to the two vectors being multiplied and the plane containing them. This only has meaning in 3D space. The magnitude of the cross product is the area of the parallelogram that the two vectors span. The direction of the cross product is orthogonal to both vectors, and follows the right-hand rule.

Note
Cross product is not commutative, is anti-commutative, meaning that a x b = -b x a.
